## Design Context

### Users
Primary audience is mixed and equally important: recruiters, clients, students, and developer peers.
The portfolio should quickly establish credibility, communicate outcomes, and make it easy to contact or verify work.
Users often scan first, then drill down into proof (projects, achievements, links, and API data).

### Brand Personality
Minimalistic, clean, and medium-technical.
Tone should feel warm and human first, then professional and precise.
Voice should be confident without sounding hype-heavy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm human + professional with a black-and-white foundation.
Use subtle warm neutrals (off-black and off-white) rather than pure #000/#fff.
Support both light and dark themes with system auto behavior and manual toggle.
Keep existing product behaviors (mode picker, shader background, chatbot), but refresh visuals, spacing, copy, interaction states, and accessibility quality.

### Design Principles
1. Clarity before decoration: strong hierarchy, concise copy, and obvious next actions.
2. Monochrome with warmth: black/white-led palette with tasteful neutral tinting and high contrast.
3. Motion with purpose: restrained transitions for context, not distraction; honor reduced-motion preferences.
4. Accessibility as baseline: target WCAG AAA contrast and keyboard-visible focus across core interactions.
5. Progressive depth: simple entry points with richer detail available through exploration.
